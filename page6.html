Pagadian Plaza luz
PreviousNextClose

The original name of PLAZA LUZ was "Plaza Morgan" named after Guerrilla leader Col. Luis Morgan.

Col. Luis Morgan was the Chief of Staff of Col. Wendell Fertig (who commanded the guerrilla forces that held the island of Mindanao) , a Filipino American mestizo, that gave Col. Fertig the idea to promote himself to one-star general making him the highest-ranking American military officer in Mindanao. Morgan had convinced him that it would encourage more Filipinos to join.

Morgan immediately spread a lie throughout the island that an American general had just arrived from Australia by submarine to take command of Mindanao. As a result, many guerilla units sprung up and sought to be part of Fertig’s command of 10th Military District. It was a huge boost to Fertig’s plan to lead the resistance movement in Mindanao.

In the meantime, MacArthur’s headquarters chose not to question Fertig’s self promotion, because they had a plan to use some of Fertig’s personnel to perform “coast watches” which were needed when MacArthur returned to the Philippines in the future.

The local Government of Pagadian named the Plaza after Col. Morgan’s because of his heroism and became a big part of the Japanese Liberation here in Mindanao.

During the time of Mayor Felimon Pajares, the plaza was named after his first lady Mrs.Luz Pajares. Plaza Luz became the symbol of pride and heroism of our fore fathers that lead us to live peacefully. 

Now you know the History of Plaza Luz. As a Pagadianon, I must do my part to protect and preserve the Plaza Luz by doing what is right and just(help cleaning its surrounding). Let’s do this all together hand in hand.
